SOLVE ONE SIDE MODULUS (<) INEQUALITY


QUESTION: \(| \frac{1}{x - 2} | < 3\)


Explanation Solution
1. Apply Formula \(| x | < a ↔ -a < x < a\) \(-3 < \frac{1}{x - 2} < 3\)
2. \( x > -a \) and \( x < a \) \( \frac{1}{x - 2} > -3\)

and

\( \frac{1}{x - 2} < 3\)
3. Zerorise RHS and simplify the inequality \( \frac{1}{x - 2} + 3 > 0\)

and

\( \frac{1}{x - 2} - 3 < 0\)
4. Factorise completely \( \frac{3x - 5}{x - 2} > 0\)

and

\( \frac{7 - 3x}{x - 2} < 0\)
5. Find all critical values \( x = \frac{5}{3} ; x = 2\)

and

\( x = \frac{7}{3} ; x = 2\)
6. Table of Sign

and
7. Write down the solution \( (-∞ , \frac{5}{3} )\) U \((2 , ∞)\) and \((-∞ , 2)\) U \((\frac{7}{3} , ∞)\)
8. Use number lines to merge the answers
9. Write down the final solution \( (-∞ , \frac{5}{3})\) U \((\frac{7}{3} , ∞)\)
