SOLVE BOTH MODULUS INEQUALITY



QUESTION: \(| x - 3 | > 2x + 5\)


Explanation Solution
1. Squaring both sides to eliminate modulus \((x - 3)^2 > (2x + 5)^2\)
2. Expand all terms \( x^2 - 6x + 9 > 4x^2 + 20x + 25\)
3. Zerorise RHS and simplify the inequality \( 3x^2 + 26x + 16 > 0\)
4. Factorise completely \( (3x + 2)(x + 8) > 0\)
5. Find all critical values \( x = -\frac{2}{3}\)

or

\(x = 2\)
6. Table of Sign
7. Write down the solution \( (-∞ , -8 )\) U \((-\frac{2}{3} , ∞)\)
